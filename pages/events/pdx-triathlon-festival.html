Triathlon FAQs

Are wetsuits allowed/required?
Wetsuits are allowed and recommended! Water temperature is typically 65-70¬∞F in early June. Wetsuits are optional but can provide warmth and buoyancy. If water temp exceeds 78¬∞F, wetsuits are prohibited for age group awards per USAT rules.
What are the transition area rules?
The transition area opens at 6:00 AM on race day. You'll be assigned a spot by bib number. Rack your bike by the seat, helmet on handlebars. Only athletes are allowed in transition‚Äîno outside assistance. We'll have a practice transition open on Saturday afternoon!
What type of bike can I use?
Any road-safe bicycle is welcome‚Äîroad bikes, triathlon bikes, hybrids, even mountain bikes! No recumbents, tandems, or e-bikes. Bikes must have working brakes and bar-end plugs. Helmets are MANDATORY and must be CPSC/ASTM certified.
I'm a beginner‚Äîis this event for me?
Absolutely! We designed PDX Tri Festival to be the most beginner-friendly triathlon in the Pacific Northwest. Our Sprint distance is perfect for first-timers, and we offer a free pre-race clinic covering swim, bike, run, and transition tips. You've got this!
Can I do a relay team?
Yes! Both Sprint and Olympic distances offer relay options. Form a team of 2-3 athletes‚Äîone swims, one bikes, one runs (or split however you like). Great option for those new to multi-sport!
What's the Kids Tri like?
The Kids Tri is held Saturday afternoon with age-appropriate distances for ages 7-15. Pool swim in a safe environment, short bike loop with traffic-free roads, and a fun run to the finish. Every kid gets a medal and feels like a champion!
