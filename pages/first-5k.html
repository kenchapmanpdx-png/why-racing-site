You're Not Alone

That Voice In Your Head? Everyone Hears It.

The worries we hear most—and the truth that might surprise you.

"What if I'm the slowest one there?"
Reality: Many people walk the entire thing. The only person tracking your time is you.
"What if I have to walk?"
Reality: Walking isn't cheating—it's smart pacing. At our events, it's encouraged.
"I'm not a 'real runner'"
Reality: If you run, you're a runner. No minimum speed or body type required.
"What if I can't finish?"
Reality: If you can walk 45 minutes, you can finish. Our courses stay open.
"I don't know what to do"
Reality: We send everything you need to know—no surprises.
"People will judge me"
Reality: Runners cheer loudest for the back of the pack. You'll feel welcomed.

Your Training Plan

8-Week Couch to 5K

Three days a week. That's all it takes.

This plan uses run/walk intervals that gradually build your endurance. By Week 8, you'll be ready to run 30 minutes—enough to finish any 5K.

Day 1 Alternate 60s jog / 90s walk × 8
Day 2 Alternate 60s jog / 90s walk × 8
Day 3 Alternate 60s jog / 90s walk × 8
Tip: Go slower than you think you should. If you can't talk, slow down.
Day 1 Alternate 90s jog / 2min walk × 6
Day 2 Alternate 90s jog / 2min walk × 6
Day 3 Alternate 90s jog / 2min walk × 6
Tip: Rest days matter. Your body builds strength while recovering.
Day 1 90s jog, 90s walk, 3min jog, 3min walk × 2
Day 2 Repeat Day 1
Day 3 Repeat Day 1
Tip: 3 minutes will feel long. That's normal. You can do this.
Day 1 3min jog, 90s walk, 5min jog, 2.5min walk, 3min, 90s, 5min
Day 2 Repeat Day 1
Day 3 Repeat Day 1
Tip: Halfway there! You've built real endurance.
Day 1 5min jog, 3min walk, 5min jog, 3min walk, 5min jog
Day 2 8min jog, 5min walk, 8min jog
Day 3 20min jog — no walking!
Tip: Day 3 is a milestone. Go slow. You're ready for this.
Day 1 5min jog, 3min walk, 8min jog, 3min walk, 5min jog
Day 2 10min jog, 3min walk, 10min jog
Day 3 22min jog
Tip: You're a runner now. Let that sink in.
Day 1 25min jog
Day 2 25min jog
Day 3 25min jog
Tip: Find your pace. You should be able to hold a conversation.
Day 1 28min jog
Day 2 28min jog
Day 3 30min jog — You did it!
Tip: You're ready for race day. Trust your training!
